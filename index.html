    Why OptiHK

    Large AI needs a cleaner path than copper and power-hungry SERDES.

    Electrical reach, package density, and thermal pressure are becoming system-level limits. OptiHK moves optical I/O closer to compute so bandwidth can scale with shorter electrical paths and denser optical channels.

    Interconnect bottlenecks

    • Copper GPU links face loss, reach, cable bulk, and signal-integrity limits as bandwidth density rises.
    • Retimers and high-power SERDES chips add system power, thermal load, latency, and board complexity.
    • Dense AI racks need more channels without filling packages and boards with long electrical paths.

    OptiHK Optical I/O

    • Move high-speed data optically at the package edge while shortening difficult electrical traces.
    • Use compact silicon photonic interfaces to reduce interconnect power and latency pressure.
    • Increase data-channel density through efficient coupling, modulation, and multiplexing.
